MERFISH Preoptic Region

Our exploratory effort develops novel and effective visualization tools using Altair to aid research investigation and hypothesis synthesis about social behaviours and their anatomical and genetic underpinnings, enabling succinct, reliable, and relevant exploratory data analysis (EDA) of the hypothalamic preoptic region data for enhanced understanding of neuronal diversity, sexual dimorphism, and social behaviors.

Moffitt & Bambah-Mukku et al. (2018) | Read the Paper | Dataset | Read our Final Report

01 – Spatial Mapping to Brain Atlas

Maps cells to a mouse brain atlas, revealing origins in ventral, bottom brain regions like Medial Preoptic Necleus (MPOC), medial part of Medial Preoptic (MPOM), and the Medial Preoptic Area (MPA), providing nuanced anatomical insights within the POA across behaviors and key social genes.

Additional Notes

This novel and perhaps unprecedented Altair-based genetic-level brain mapping enhances understanding of spatial organization.

Investigates the relationship between variance explained by sex and by individual experimental mice IDs, noting no clear pattern but some outliers suggesting potential positive associations.

Additional Notes

This analysis demands further nuance, aligning with the paper's emphasis on individual variability in neuromodulatory signatures across a subset of ~10,000 profiled cells.

02 – Gene Expression Variability Between Sexes

Analyzes variability in gene expression between sexes, with Esr1 (female-dominated Estrogen gene) showing greater variability in males, and Avpr1a (male-specific Vasopressin gene) higher in males, implying behavioral-genetic interactions.

Additional Notes

Supports the original paper's insights into sex hormone signaling and neuromodulators like Vasopressin (Avp) in social behaviors.
